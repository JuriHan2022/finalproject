POLLINATION

The project tries to encapsulate and observe the process of organic evolution of flowers through randomly regenerated mechanics of coding. The system is automated but every part of the code is interdependent and I can only merely try to understand why the numbers make such creations but cannot control — like it should be for all natural objects. Special Thanks to Kazuki Umeda who's video helped me figure out this code.

Creation and its speed in nature while seems so capturable at times is so unsightly done. Like electricity the fragments of growth are at such detail at a pace we cannot capture with our raw eyes as it occurs all together with a high degree of interdependence. 
‍
At night you see a flower bud peeking its head above the branch, and the next morning with beautiful sunshine blooms like it was in its state forever. We just missed its small buds expanding, leaf slowly unraveling reaching towards the sky while colors are actively fleshing through its petals. 
‍
If we could capture the unsighted activities of these flowers - growing harmoniously like a perfectly synchronized orchestra during the times of our sleep — this would be the true encapsulation of the blooming mechanical age. 
‍
But if all these flowers were an orchestra — would they play the same tune? Akin to these flowers our growths as humans are unsighted and interdependent. We expand and bloom in parts and areas that we are not able to quite understand until time has passed enough to see the whole picture. 
‍
The project tries to encapsulate and observe the process of organic evolution through a randomly regenerated mechanical system of coding. The system is automated but every part of the code is interdependent and I can only merely try to understand why the numbers make such creations but cannot control — as it should be for all-natural objects.
‍
What is your vision of the mechanical age? Are we to bloom or to die? To be more connected or to be more separated? “Nomadic gatherers of knowledge, free from fragmentary specialisms” or merely a particle of a hugely controlled system?
